Donut Facts

When fried dough was introduced to New Amsterdam (now New York), early Americans took the fact that the treats were fried in oil quite literally, naming them olykoeks, translating to "oily cakes."
A Russian man named Adolph Levitt invented the donut machine which launched donut production from local bakeries to mass production facilities, leading to a boom in donut popularity and sales.
Vernon Rudolph bought the secret recipe for the Krispy Kreme donut from a frenchmen in New Orleans and brought back the recipe to North Carolina where Krispy Kreme Donuts was born.
The biggest donut ever made was an American-style jelly donut, weighing in at 1.7 tons and measuring 1 foot tall.
